# Adversarial review — U-ST1.1 REBUILT endurance study (commit a1ed64a vs main)

## Verdict summary
The **headline is honest** (km-scale denied endurance, 500 m not met, reproducible, geometry genuinely good — the D65 MEO confound is properly fixed). But the study does not stop at the honest headline: it commits to a **specific causal diagnosis (per-SV bias re-convergence across handover) that is asserted, not evidenced, and is in tension with its own data**, and it justifies one of its two fixture changes with an **empirically false coverage claim** that also defies the explicit D65 reuse mandate. By the brief's stated PASS bar (diagnosis must be supported; generator fixes must be legitimate), this fails on two of the three conditions.

## Mechanical gates (all PASS)
- `cargo fmt --check` clean; `cargo clippy --workspace --all-targets -D warnings` clean; all 6 endurance tests + full `pnt-studies` suite (21) pass.
- Reproduced with `--release`: `results.json` and `STUDY.md` are **byte-identical** to committed. Deterministic.
- Real `Executive`+`FilterStub` EKF vs generator truth; production gate `Some(9.0)`; rejection counts reported. No clamp/formula/target-fit; the study reports FAILURE to reach 500 m (anti-target-fit — good).
- **Scope clean**: `pnt-mission`, `pnt-estimator`, `fusion-executive`, `multisat.rs`, `highspeed.rs`, `passage.rs` all show **zero diff vs main**. The two "generator changes" are config-level in `endurance.rs` only (`wave_slam: None`, vertical `imu_bias` = 0, local 768-SV `synthetic_fixture`). Other studies are unaffected. No attribution trailers in commit a1ed64a.

## (a) HEADLINE — geometry genuinely ruled out (HONEST)
GDOP is real and well-conditioned. Reproduced values: mean 2.11→2.73 (10→60 min), min 1.47, max 3.06→7.29 — computed by a standard `(HᵀH)⁻¹` trace over the tracked cohort's LOS (`endurance.rs:873`). Comparable to multisat's ~1.8. Geometry is genuinely NOT the cause of km-scale — the D65 confound (MEO 7.5× slow-sky) is fixed; `fixture_is_leo_not_meo` guards it. **This is the strong part of the work.**
- Minor caveat [LOW]: GDOP is *pseudorange* geometry, but this is a Doppler-only filter whose position observability is governed by geometry *change*, not instantaneous GDOP. "GDOP good → geometry not the limiter" rules out the ill-conditioned confound (fine) but GDOP is not the true observability governor here — multisat itself notes this (`multisat.rs:711`, "per-SV nuisance biases make actual observability weaker than GDOP alone"), yet endurance leans on GDOP as its geometry-control proof.

## (b) PER-SV-BIAS-ACROSS-HANDOVER DIAGNOSIS — ASSERTED, NOT EVIDENCED [CRITICAL / high confidence]
- **File/claim**: `endurance.rs:708-711` (and `results.json:346`) states as fact: *"the fixed-cohort 116 m / 554 m does NOT transfer to sustained endurance because continuous handover keeps per-SV bias observability from converging."* The commit message and STUDY.md repeat it and build the project-steering recommendation on it ("resolve per-SV bias under handover churn, not longer legs / not better clock").
- **Mechanism is real in code but never isolated**: the estimator does augment a per-SV nuisance bias at variance 100 (`fusion-executive/src/lib.rs:321` → `pnt-estimator/src/lib.rs:163`) and **never retires it** (`retire_satellite_bias` is used only in estimator unit tests, never in the pipeline). So a fresh handover SV does introduce an unconverged 10-m/s-σ bias state. Plausible — but plausibility is not evidence.
- **None of the brief's evidence tests exist**: the study collects only a *single-epoch endpoint* error, per-epoch GDOP, and a handover *count*. There is no error-vs-epoch trace, no error spike aligned to handover epochs, no correlation of error with handover recency, and no bias-state covariance at the endpoint. A bias-zeroed control run and a fixed-cohort long-leg control (the two experiments that would isolate bias-across-handover from the simpler "Doppler-only weak observability of a slow 7-kn receiver + prior decay") are both absent.
- **The aggregate data runs OPPOSITE to the diagnosis**: handovers rise 10→20→32→48→65 across the leg tiers while p95 error *falls* 31520→17552→24962→19649→9251 m (`results.json` leg curve). If handover-induced bias re-convergence were the dominant driver, more handovers would not systematically *reduce* error. Longer integration clearly dominates; the handover-bias effect is at best second-order and the study never separates "longer legs help (integration)" from "handover hurts (bias)". The refined recency claim ("endpoint samples instantaneous handover geometry") is also unshown.
- **How to verify**: re-run one seed with `sv_bias_hz` forced to 0 — if endpoint error collapses toward multisat-class, bias is the driver; if it stays km-scale, the limiter is fundamental Doppler-only observability and the diagnosis is wrong. Neither this nor a per-epoch error trace is in the artifact.

## (c-ii) GRID CHANGE — justification EMPIRICALLY FALSE [MEDIUM / high confidence]
- **Claim** (`endurance.rs:4-8`, STUDY.md, commit): the 960-SV multisat grid *"leaves instantaneous equatorial coverage gaps over an hour,"* so it was "densified" to 768 SVs.
- **Verified false**: I reconstructed the exact multisat 960-SV grid (16 planes × 20 slots × 3 shells) and measured visibility over the full 60-min leg at the same equatorial origin: **min 22 visible, 0 epochs below 8** — *better* than the endurance 768 grid's **min 18**. The 960 grid has no coverage gap. D65 explicitly mandated *"use the multisat LEO synthetic fixture (960 SVs keeps 45-54 visible continuously)"* — the study contradicted that mandate with an unsupported claim and built a different grid.
- "Densified" is a misnomer: 768 < 960 (fewer slots/plane: 16 vs 20). The change is **result-neutral** (both grids well-conditioned, continuous coverage), so it is NOT result-engineering toward the km-scale number — but the stated reason is factually wrong and un-mandated.
- **How to verify**: the throwaway coverage test I ran (min-visible over 60 min for both grids) — 960→min 22, 768→min 18. (Test removed; worktree clean.)

## (c-i) WAVE-SLAM DISABLE — legitimate honest bug-fix [PASS]
The 1 Hz aliasing is mathematically real: `wave_slam_acceleration` (`pnt-mission/src/lib.rs:641`) uses `phase = (seconds mod duration_s)/duration_s`; at integer seconds with the canonical `duration_s = 0.25` (confirmed real — `highspeed.rs:229`), `phase ≡ 0`, so `cos(0)=+1` on every fired burst → strictly-upward DC drift, destroying the zero-mean property the model relies on (which the mission's own test verifies only at 1 ms resolution, `lib.rs:808`). The ~6 m/s²/~1187 km figures are order-consistent with `burst_rate 0.08 × duration 0.25` firing probability. Disabling at 1 Hz cadence is correct and **conservative** — it makes truth *more* benign (smoother), which would *understate* error, so it cannot inflate the bad-news headline. Zeroing vertical IMU bias (`endurance.rs:360`) only keeps truth physical; `horizontal_error` uses east/north only, so it hides nothing in the reported metric. Fully disclosed. Legitimate.

## (d) Coherence with U-RT1.1 [consistent]
The short-leg realtle 36-52 m (DR-coast-flattered) vs long-leg km-scale tells one coherent story: the initial aided fix / prior carries a short leg, and only after it decays over 10-60 min does true Doppler-only km-scale emerge (endurance already shows p50 8552 m at the 10-min tier). No contradiction. (Note: the fixed-cohort→handover attribution for the 116 m→km-scale gap is entangled with prior-decay — see finding b.)

## (e) Honesty guardrails
- Gate on, rejection counts real, real EKF, no clamp/formula/target-fit, reports failure — all good.
- Bimodal 60-min honestly characterized: 5/16 seeds ≤500 m, p50=5067 m headlined as the failure (not the 33 m best-case) — anti-cherry-pick, good. [LOW caveat] both the headline and the bimodality rest on a *single-epoch endpoint* metric of a noisy Doppler-only solution; a time-averaged/RMS-over-leg metric is not reported, so the bimodality may be partly a sampling artifact — the study even explains the 30-min non-monotone tier as an endpoint-sampling artifact, conceding the metric is noisy.
- [LOW] Doc/code inconsistency in the scrutinized area: `synthetic_fixture` comment says "960 satellites" but generates 768 (`endurance.rs:822`).

## Verdict rationale
Geometry is genuinely ruled out and the km-scale headline is honest, reproducible, and conservative — a real, important, well-supported finding *that 500 m is not met*. But the brief's PASS bar requires all three: honest headline **and** legitimate generator fixes **and** a supported diagnosis. Two fail: (1) the per-SV-bias-across-handover diagnosis — the study's committed causal explanation and the basis of its next-phase recommendation — is asserted without any isolating control or handover-aligned evidence, and its own aggregate data (error falls as handovers rise) cuts against it; (2) the grid change is justified by an empirically false coverage claim and defies the explicit D65 mandate to reuse the 960-SV grid. Neither is result-engineering of the headline number, and both are narrowly fixable (add a bias-zeroed control + per-epoch/handover-aligned error trace to evidence or retract the diagnosis; revert to the 960 grid or drop the false justification) — but as committed, the study over-claims its cause and mis-justifies a fixture change, exactly the class of issue this seat exists to catch.

FAIL
