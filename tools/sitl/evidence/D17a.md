# D17a GPS_INPUT-silence characterisation

Environment: pinned Rover-4.6.3 (`3fc7011a7d3dc047cbb17d8bd98ee94577d144c6`),
boat frame, external MAVLink GPS, armed GUIDED (custom mode 15), `FS_EKF_ACTION=1` default
(HOLD). Times below are relative to the recorded `STREAM_STOP` event.

## Native Rover response — VERIFIED

- At t=0 the vehicle was armed in GUIDED, EKF flags were 831 (absolute horizontal position,
  no constant-position mode), throttle was 0, and servo outputs 1/3 were 1500/1500.
- `GPS_RAW_INT.fix_type` changed from 3 to 1 at t=+1.019 s.
- EKF absolute horizontal position disappeared at t=+4.212 s (flags 39).
- At t=+5.071 s telemetry simultaneously showed armed custom mode 4 (HOLD), `EKF variance`,
  and `EKF failsafe`. It remained armed. Throttle stayed 0 and servo 1/3 stayed 1500/1500.
- Both EKF lanes reported `stopped aiding` at t=+12.141 s; flags later became 167
  (constant-position mode).

Representative raw records are in `d17a-mavlink.jsonl`; the complete run contains 2738
telemetry/event records. Source-level cause: `AP_GPS.cpp` declares `GPS_TIMEOUT_MS=4000` and
marks a timed-out receiver no-fix; `Rover/ekf_check.cpp` requires good absolute/relative
position while armed, debounces failure for 10 iterations at 10 Hz, and applies HOLD when
`FS_EKF_ACTION` is HOLD and the current mode requires position.

## Case-A companion HOLD candidate — VERIFIED

A separate run sent SET_MODE HOLD 1.531 s after stream stop, while Rover was still armed in
GUIDED. HEARTBEAT changed from custom mode 15 at t=+1.532 s to custom mode 4 at t=+1.583 s:
51 ms observed command-to-mode latency, about 3.49 s earlier than the native failsafe in the
baseline run. Outputs stayed neutral. Later `EKF variance` was reported, but there was no
`EKF failsafe` text because HOLD does not require position and Rover's event handler returns
without that action/text. Full telemetry is in `d17a-case-a-mavlink.jsonl`.
